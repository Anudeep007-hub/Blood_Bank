General criteria

  • Be between the ages of 18 and 65.
  • Have a hemoglobin level of at least 12.5 g/dL.
  • Have a pulse between 50 and 100 beats per minute.
  • Have a blood pressure of 100-180 mm Hg systolic and 50-100 mm Hg diastolic.
  • Have a normal body temperature, with an oral temperature not exceeding 37.5 Â°C.
  • Not have tuberculosis, leprosy, hepatitis B or C, or HIV.
  • Not have any heart disease or ailment.

    Learn about blood

    Blood is a vital fluid that courses through the human body, carrying out a myriad of essential functions necessary for life. Composed of various cells, proteins, and nutrients suspended in a liquid called plasma, blood plays a critical role in maintaining the body's equilibrium and ensuring its proper functioning. One of its primary functions is transportation, as it delivers oxygen from the lungs to every cell in the body and carries carbon dioxide back to the lungs for exhalation. Additionally, blood transports nutrients absorbed from the digestive system to tissues, organs, and cells, providing them with the energy and building blocks necessary for growth, repair, and maintenance.

    Beyond its role in transportation, blood also serves crucial roles in defense and regulation. White blood cells, a key component of blood, form the backbone of the immune system, defending the body against harmful pathogens such as bacteria, viruses, and fungi. Blood also helps regulate body temperature, pH levels, and fluid balance, ensuring optimal conditions for cellular function. Moreover, blood clotting mechanisms prevent excessive bleeding and promote wound healing, safeguarding the body against life-threatening hemorrhages. In essence, blood is the life force that sustains human existence, underscoring its profound importance in maintaining health and vitality.

    Benefits of blood donation

  • Save lives: In India, millions require blood transfusions every year due to accidents, surgeries, childbirth complications, blood disorders like thalassemia, and more. A single donation can help up to three people, making a massive impact.
  • Strengthens Your Community: Blood banks in India often face shortages. By donating, you directly contribute to the local supply, ensuring life-saving blood is available for those in your community.
  • Quick Health Check: Before each donation, you receive a mini-health screening that checks temperature, blood pressure, pulse, and hemoglobin. This can potentially identify underlying health issues early on.
  • Boosts Your Health: Studies suggest regular blood donation, while maintaining iron levels, may reduce the risk of heart disease and hemochromatosis (iron overload).
  • Feel-Good Factor: Donating blood is a selfless act that brings a sense of satisfaction and accomplishment. Knowing you've potentially saved lives can be incredibly rewarding.
  • Builds New Blood Cells: Your body is a remarkable machine! After a donation, it ramps up production of new red blood cells, keeping your circulatory system healthy.
  • Rare Blood Type Advantage: If you have a rare blood type, your donation becomes even more critical in India, where specific blood types are constantly in high demand.
  • Manages Iron Levels: For people with conditions like hemochromatosis, regular blood donation can help regulate iron levels in the body, preventing complications.
  • Convenient and Time-Efficient: The donation process itself is relatively quick, taking about an hour. Many blood donation camps are organized throughout India for added convenience.
  • Contributes to Medical Advancement: Blood donation programs in India sometimes involve research initiatives. Your contribution could potentially aid in developing new treatments or diagnostics.
